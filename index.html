Project Moonstone

Scrape through clinical databases and uncover hidden relationshps in health data.

Here's the thing

The 21st buzzwords of IoT and big-data are used so often and interchangably the average person tends to forget they're actual things that can help society. While their applications span across any-and-all industries (both technical and humanitarian), one area that excites me especially is Mobile Health, wearable technology, and remote patient monitoring (See the Health Data wiki page). All three of these areas have one thing in common: they create patient data. In this demonstration, witness first-hand how large sets of health metrics can help researchers glean insights about health trends and relationships.

You're the reseacher. Pick two health metrics below and discover any potential relationships among them. The program will access HealthData.gov's Database, gather the data, and display it for you in a nice little plot. See if you can uncover any hidden relationships! (Hint: The higher the R2 value, the more related the two variables are!)

Select a health database and metric selection

First, use the dropdown menu to select which database you would like to sift though. It's contents will load onto a server, then you get to choose which two health attributes to analyze. For example, say you select the MIMIC-II database and want to analze patient's Cardiac Out and their height (Maybe there's an un-anticipated relationship?!). Simply Select the database, then those both traits! Hit "Run" and see what you discover!
